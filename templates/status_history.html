Application Lifecycle

Every job application goes through several stages. Understanding where each application stands helps you prioritize your time and follow up effectively.

Applied

You have submitted your application. The company is reviewing your materials. Tip: Record the date and follow up within 1–2 weeks if you haven't heard back.

Phone Screen

A recruiter or hiring manager has reached out for an initial conversation. Prepare a quick summary of your background and questions about the role.

Interview Scheduled

You have a formal interview lined up. Research the company, prepare your answers, and confirm details like format, interviewers, and logistics.

Offer Received

Congratulations! You have received a job offer. Take time to review the terms, compensation, and benefits before responding or negotiating.

Rejected

The company has decided not to move forward. Consider requesting feedback, reflect on areas to improve, and keep applying — rejection is a normal part of the process.

Withdrawn

You have chosen to withdraw your application. It's always courteous to formally notify the employer and keep the door open for future opportunities.
